🎯 8 Essential Professional Phrasal Verbs

Mastering informal fluency in the workplace.

Why Phrasal Verbs Are Critical

In professional English, especially in spoken meetings, presentations, and internal communication, **phrasal verbs** are used more frequently than their single-word equivalents (e.g., *deal with* instead of *manage*).

🗣️ **Sound Natural and Fluent:** Using phrasal verbs shows that you understand the common, day-to-day language of native speakers. It's the difference between sounding like a translated text and sounding like a colleague.

📧 **Communicate Effectively:** Many phrasal verbs (like **Sort Out**) express complex ideas simply and concisely. Being able to recognize and use them prevents misunderstandings and makes your speech more direct and efficient.

🇧🇷 Por Que Usamos Phrasal Verbs? Uma Perspectiva Brasileira

Para quem fala português, a lógica dos phrasal verbs (verbos + preposição/advérbio) é parecida com a nossa preferência por expressões do dia a dia.

✨ **É mais natural, não é?** No Brasil, você provavelmente diz "**Se vira** com esse problema" em vez de "Solucione este problema" ou "**Dar conta** do trabalho" em vez de "Gerenciar o trabalho."

🗣️ **Menos Formal:** Phrasal verbs como **Sort Out** ou **Deal With** soam mais casuais e diretos em inglês. Usar o verbo simples (como *Manage* ou *Resolve*) pode ser correto, mas muitas vezes soa mais formal e menos como uma conversa de rotina no escritório. Use-os para ganhar **fluência conversacional**!

The 8 Verbs You Need to Master

1. Deal with 🤝

Meaning: To manage or handle a situation; to try to find a solution.

✅ Professional:

"We need to hire an external consultant to **deal with** the new compliance audit."

🏠 Personal:

"It's tough to **deal with** traffic every morning on the Marginal Pinheiros."

2. Sort out 🧹

Meaning: To successfully find a solution to a problem; to resolve or organize something.

✅ Professional:

"Before the launch, I must **sort out** all the bugs in the application code."

🏠 Personal:

"Can you **sort out** your closet? It's a huge mess!"

3. Come up 🚨

Meaning: To arise or appear, usually unexpectedly (referring to an issue or problem).

✅ Professional:

"A high-priority client request has just **come up**, so we must pause the current task."

🏠 Personal:

"Something unexpected **came up**, and I won't be able to go to the barbecue."

4. Come up with 💡

Meaning: To think of an idea, a solution, or a plan.

✅ Professional:

"The marketing team needs to **come up with** a disruptive campaign by the end of the week."

🏠 Personal:

"I was struggling to **come up with** a good gift idea for my mother's birthday."

5. Put off 🗓️

Meaning: To delay dealing with something; to postpone or procrastinate.

✅ Professional:

"Since the venue isn't ready, we'll have to **put off** the conference until next quarter."

🏠 Personal:

"I always **put off** doing the dishes until the sink is full."

6. Give up 🛑

Meaning: To stop trying or abandon an effort.

✅ Professional:

"We should **give up** on using that old software; it's slowing the whole team down."

🏠 Personal:

"It took me six months to quit smoking, but I eventually **gave up**."

7. Sleep on 🌙

Meaning: To delay making a decision until the next day; to think about it overnight.

✅ Professional:

"This investment is too big. Let's **sleep on it** and decide tomorrow morning."

🏠 Personal:

"Don't sign that lease agreement tonight; **sleep on it** first and read the fine print."

8. Go through 🔎

Meaning: To examine or analyze options or details one by one; to review thoroughly.

✅ Professional:

"The legal department must **go through** every clause of the contract before signing it."

🏠 Personal:

"I need to **go through** all my old photos before deleting them from my computer."

---

Actionable Advice: Using Them in Your Work 🚀

  • During Problem Solving: When an issue arises, don't just say "we have a problem." Say, "**A critical issue has come up**—let's find a way to **sort it out** quickly."
  • In Decision Making: When facing a major decision, tell your team, "**Let's sleep on it** before we decide," or "Before we start, we need to **go through** all the client feedback."
  • In Planning: If you need new ideas, ask, "Who can **come up with** a better solution?" If a task is not urgent, instruct a team member to "**put off** that delivery until Friday."
